# VO recording script — "The Line"

Narration for the eight blocks, verbatim from `script.md`, with ElevenLabs-style
delivery cues in **[square brackets]**. Record one file per block, named
`VO-01.mp3` … `VO-08.mp3`, and drop them into
`the-line/remotion/public/audio/` to replace the silent placeholders.

**Voice direction (whole film):** a quiet, weary documentary narrator. Restrained,
never theatrical. The bracketed cues are delivery hints, not words to be spoken.

**Hard constraints (do not break):**
- The narrator **never says the word "enemy."** Only the on-screen card does (S3).
- **VO-05 must finish by 1:52** — the final 8 seconds of the War section are
  wind/music only, holding on the rows of crosses.
- The final card ("NO LINE IS WORTH A GENERATION.") is **on-screen text only** —
  no narration over it.

**Recording tips:**
- Leave ~0.3s of silence at head and tail of each file; the edit places each block
  at its cue and ducks the music under it.
- Keep each block at or under its target duration below. If VO-05 runs long,
  re-record rather than compress — don't eat into the silent ending.

---

## VO-01 — Before · target ≤ 20s (0:00–0:20)

> [gentle, reflective] Before the war, there were no heroes. [soft] No enemies.
>
> [conversational] Just people who woke too early, worked too long, and tried to get home before dinner.
>
> [warm] On both sides, life was ordinary. [quiet, sincere] And that was enough.

## VO-02 — The line · target ≤ 20s (0:20–0:40)

> [measured] Then someone drew a line.
>
> [flat, matter-of-fact] No mountain stood there. No river.
>
> [slowing] Just a line, [beat] and a story about the people beyond it.

## VO-03 — The story spreads · target ≤ 22s (0:40–1:02)

> [low, uneasy] The story was repeated until fear felt like knowledge.
>
> [firmer] Questions became disloyalty.
>
> [grave, deliberate] And the people across the line stopped being people. [cold] They became one word.

## VO-04 — The machinery · target ≤ 26s (1:02–1:28)

> [steady, building] Fear became a speech. [clipped] The speech became an order. [harder] The order became a uniform.
>
> [somber, slower] And frightened people were sent to kill other frightened people.

## VO-05 — War · MUST END BY 1:52 (window ~24s, 1:28–1:52)

> [hollow, restrained] The line swallowed names, [beat] and returned numbers.
>
> [weary] Each death justified the next.
>
> [quiet resignation] Soon, no one remembered how it began. [near-whisper] Only that too many had died to stop.

## VO-06 — Victory · target ≤ 22s (2:00–2:22)

> [tired irony] Eventually, the leaders met.
>
> [pointed, bitter] They used words that had been available from the beginning.
>
> [flat] Both sides announced victory.
>
> [cold, final] The dead did not object.

## VO-07 — The cost · target ≤ 20s (2:22–2:42)

> [hushed] The line moved a few centimetres.
>
> [heavy, slow] Parents buried children.
>
> [aching] Children inherited medals, debts, and stories about people they had never met.

## VO-08 — Ending · target ≤ 14s (2:42–~2:56, leaving room for the hand, white cut, and card)

> [gentle, searching] If peace was always how it would end...
>
> [soft, almost pleading] why could it not have been how it began?

*(Then: silent hand-hesitates beat → hard cut to white → final card holds 3s → fade.)*
