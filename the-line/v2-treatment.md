# V2 Treatment — "The Line" (The Red Thread cut)

Complete visual revamp. Only the narration (`VO-01..08.mp3`, placement unchanged) and
`bgm.mp3` (40% ceiling) are retained. All visuals are new.

## Art direction

**Style:** expressive charcoal and ink illustration — smudged graphite texture, dramatic
soft light, deep blacks, aged warm-grey paper tone, cinematic wide compositions, filmic
atmosphere. Think hand-drawn animated documentary (Waltz with Bashir's gravity, drawn in
charcoal). **Generated images contain NO color whatsoever.**

**The one idea that carries the film:** a single continuous RED LINE (the locked
`RED` constant, Remotion-composited, subtly animated — never baked into images) appears
in every chapter and transforms: pencil stroke → border across the valley → barbed wire
→ orator's sash → signature stroke on orders → uniform stitching → railway track →
trench scar → horizon behind the crosses → treaty signature → the ghost+new border on
the map → a slack thread in the rubble → picked up by a child → a kite string rising
vertical (the border, upended, pointing at the sky). The camera never stops moving:
slow push-ins, drifts, parallax scale — stills must never feel still.

**Type moments** (Remotion, bold condensed letterpress over charcoal texture):
ENEMY (55–62s), VICTORY mirrored pair (127.3–130.4s), final card (175–178s).
